Key Characteristics

H

  • Lightest and most abundant element in the universe
  • Highly flammable gas
  • Forms water when combined with oxygen
  • Essential for acid-base reactions and fuel cells

C

  • Exists in multiple forms: diamond, graphite, graphene
  • Backbone of all organic compounds
  • Excellent conductor (graphite), hardest substance (diamond)
  • Crucial in biological molecules: proteins, DNA, carbohydrates

N

  • Makes up 78% of Earthâ€™s atmosphere
  • Key component of amino acids and DNA
  • Used in fertilizers, explosives, and cryogenics
  • Inert diatomic gas at room temperature

O

  • Supports combustion and respiration
  • Vital for most life forms on Earth
  • Component of water and many oxides
  • Highly reactive and forms compounds easily

F

  • Most electronegative and reactive element
  • Used in toothpaste to prevent cavities
  • Forms stable compounds with nearly all elements
  • Exists as a pale yellow toxic gas

P

  • Exists as white and red allotropes
  • Essential nutrient for plants
  • Used in fertilizers, detergents, and safety matches
  • Highly reactive and flammable (white phosphorus)

S

  • Bright yellow solid at room temperature
  • Found in proteins and vitamins
  • Used in sulfuric acid production
  • Burns with a blue flame and a choking odor

Cl

  • Greenish-yellow poisonous gas
  • Used in water purification and bleach
  • Forms salts like sodium chloride (table salt)
  • Highly reactive halogen

Se

  • Gray or red solid, used in electronics
  • Essential trace element in diet
  • Used in photocopiers and glass manufacturing
  • Has both metallic and non-metallic properties

Br

  • Reddish-brown liquid at room temperature
  • Used in flame retardants and photography
  • Corrosive and toxic
  • Part of halogen family, very reactive

I

  • Dark gray solid with violet vapors when heated
  • Used in antiseptics and iodized salt
  • Essential for thyroid hormone production
  • Less reactive than other halogens

At

  • Rare and radioactive halogen
  • Occurs naturally in uranium and thorium ores
  • Studied for potential in cancer treatment
  • Extremely limited availability
